Cassandra Luftspring graduated from Queen's University in 2010, winning the Medal in Music for highest academic standing. She is the recipient of numerous other awards including the Queen's Chancellor's Scholarship and the Graham George Memorial Scholarship in Composition.

An accomplished composer, Cassandra has received commissions for original works from the Toronto Children's Chorus, the Amadeus Choir, the Lachan Jewish Chamber Choir, the Arts York String Ensemble, and the Department of Women's Studies at Queen's. Other works have been performed by the Elmer Iseler Singers, the Melos Choir, and Chorus Canadiana, who presented her piece "Our Canada" at the 2001 International Children's Choral Festival in the Czech Republic. Her first large scale orchestral work, "Seeker's Ascent", was well received at the 2010 Atlantic Music Festival in Waterville, Maine.

Cassandra has worked as a music teacher privately, at the Contemporary Conservatory of Music and Arts, and at the Kingston School of Music. An aspiring choral conductor, she participated in the Choirs Ontario Conductor Training Programme, and has served as Conductor's Assistant for both the Toronto Children's Chorus and the Cantabile Choirs of Kingston. Cassandra co-founded and directed the Queen's a cappella group "Momentum", and is a founding member of "Voicecraft", a six voice a cappella group that debuted in June 2010 at Koerner Hall in Toronto. She is currently an accompanist for the Kingston Choral Society, the Kingston Chamber Choir, and the Queen's Conservatory of Music.
