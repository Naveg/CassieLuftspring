Cassandra Luftspring is completing a M.Mus degree in Choral Conducting at the University of Toronto under Dr. Hilary Apfelstadt. She has received both an Ontario Graduate Scholarship and the Joseph-Armand Bombardier CGS Master’s Scholarship (SSHRC) for her graduate work. Cassandra earned her B.Mus from Queen’s University, where she received numerous awards including the Medal in Music, the Chancellor’s Scholarship, and the Graham George Memorial Scholarship in Composition. While studying piano, composition, voice, and organ, she worked with Mark Sirett as Assistant Conductor for his Cantabile Choirs of Kingston. She also founded and directed “Momentum,” a student a cappella group.

This year she was Assistant Conductor for the University of Toronto Women’s Chamber Choir, and Conductor’s Intern for the Toronto Children’s Chorus. She recently participated in a conducting masterclass with Ivars Taurins at Podium 2012, and attended the Tafelmusik Baroque Summer Institute in June. Cassandra was also mentored by Iwan Edwards in the Choirs Ontario Conductor Training Programme.

Cassandra is an experienced music teacher and accompanist, and an accomplished composer. She has taught at the Contemporary Conservatory of Music and Arts, and the Kingston School of Music, and has accompanied for the Kingston Choral Society, the Kingston Chamber Choir, and Queen's Conservatory. Cassandra has received commissions from many organizations including the Toronto Children’s Chorus and the Amadeus Choir. She won seven consecutive first place awards in the Amadeus Choir’s Christmas Carol and Hanukah Song Writing Competition. Her works have been performed by the Elmer Iseler Singers and the DaCapo Chamber Choir, among others. Her first large scale orchestral work was well received at the 2010 Atlantic Music Festival in Waterville, Maine. Notable teachers include John Burge, Marjan Mozetich, and Derek Holman.
